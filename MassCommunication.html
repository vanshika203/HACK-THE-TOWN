MASS COMMUNICATION

Mass Communication is a study of broadcasting and structuring news in a creative way to spread it to large masses across the globe or a nation. In Mass Communication courses, students are taught to run a radio, TV channel, internet, digital media, etc. platforms effectively through subjects like Media Law and Ethics, Media Planning and Strategy, Broadcasting, Research, etc. In India, students can pursue Mass Communication Courses after 12th, 10th, as well as after graduation. Some popular Mass Communication courses are namely Certificate in Journalism, BA in Mass Communication, Diploma or PG Diploma in Mass Communication, PhD in Mass Communication, etc.


UG Courses for Mass Communication

Students who wish to pursue a career in Mass Communication have a variety of options at Diploma and Undergraduate level to choose Journalism and Mass Communication courses after 12th. The duration of Mass Communication courses after 12th is 3 years for Bachelor’s degree and 4 months to 2 years for Diploma.After completing these Undergraduate or UG Diploma level courses students are awarded with a degree in the same.

Master Courses for Mass Communication

A wide range of course options are available for students who wish to pursue Mass Communication Courses after Graduation.The Mass Communication courses after graduation are offered at PG Diploma, Master’s and PhD levels of study.The eligibility for Postgraduate and PG Diploma Mass communication courses after graduation is to hold a bachelor degree in any discipline.Whereas, for PhD in Mass Communication Courses students are required to hold a Master’s degree in Mass Communication related discipline.

Diploma and PG Diploma in Mass Communication

Diploma in Journalism and Mass Communication is a 1-year long course that involves the conduct of Advertising, Political Campaigning, Journalism processes with the use of radio, social media, television and so on. Journalism is the activity of gathering, creating, evaluating, and presenting information in the form of news and articles. To be an eligible candidate for Diploma in Journalism and Mass Communication, the aspirants must have scored a minimum of 50% marks at the 10+2 level and must have studied English as a subject and possess speaking and writing skills.
